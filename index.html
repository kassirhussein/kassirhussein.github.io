Hi, I’m Hussein Kassir — Genomic Data Scientist / Pharmacist / Researcher

I build useful things and love learning. I am a Genomic Data Scientist with a background in pharmacy and pharmacogenomics. I have a strong interest in the application of data science and machine learning to solve problems in genomics, human genetics, and drug response.

  • My research focuses on developing computational methods to understand the genetic basis of rare diseases and drug response, with a particular emphasis on integrating genomic and phenotypic data
  • This page highlights a few projects I have done, my skills, and how I work

    • I worked on multiple projects in recent years using a variety of public and private databses. For example, Integrated data from OpenTargets, IMPC, Yellow Card side-effect reports, and paralogy analysis to identify potential gene–drug target relationships.

    I analysed Genomics England's WES data of over 4000 patients recruited under the "100k Genomes Project", to identify genotype-phenotype correlations in NDDs.
