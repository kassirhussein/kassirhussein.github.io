Functional Genomics and Drug Targets

This project focuses on the usage of public genomic and phenotypic databses to better understand drug targets.

In this project, we have used the International Mouse Phenotyping Consortium (IMPC) database to stratify drug targets in multiple essentiality categories to better understand the link between gene essentiality and its effects on drug responses. I have used the Full Spectrum of Intolerance to Loss-of-Function (FUSIL), proposed by Cacheiro et al. (2020), to stratify genes/targets into five essentiality categories: Cellular Lethal (CL), Developmental Lethal (DL), Subviable (SV), Viable with Phenotype (VP), and Viable with No Phenotype (VN). We then investigated the correlation between these categories and drug development success rates, as well as their side effect profiles.

Genotype Phenotype Correlations in Neurodevelopmental disorder (NDDs)

© 2025 Genomics England

This project uses Genomic England's rare diseases cohort data to better understand genetic correlations to specific Phenotypes in patietns with NDDs.

In this project, we are investigating the correlation between specific genetic variants and phenotypic presentations in patients with neurodevelopmental disorders (NDDs). By analyzing whole-exome sequencing data alongside detailed clinical phenotypes (Described in HPO terms), we aim to identify novel genotype-phenotype correlations that could inform diagnosis and treatment strategies for patients with NDDs. This research enhanced our understanding of the genetic architecture of NDDs which could contribute to personalized medicine approaches in this field.

Bioinformatics Pipelines and Python Utilities Development

© 2025 Hussein Kassir

Developed bioinformatics pipelines and Python utilities to streamline genomic data analysis workflows.Built Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) processing pipelines using Snakemake and GATK4, automated routine analyses on Linux systems, and designed lightweight Python packages for tasks such as GC content calculation, reverse complement generation, and in-frame stop codon detection.

These tools improved data reproducibility, reduced manual workload, and enabled scalable processing of sequencing datasets across multiple projects.
