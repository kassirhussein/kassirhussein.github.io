Functional Genomics and Drug Targets

This project focuses on the usage of public genomic and phenotypic databses to better understand drug targets.

In this project, we have used the International Mouse Phenotyping Consortium (IMPC) database to stratify drug targets in multiple essentiality categories to better understand the link between gene essentiality and its effects on drug responses. I have used the Full Spectrum of Intolerance to Loss-of-Function (FUSIL), proposed by Cacheiro et al. (2020), to stratify genes/targets into five essentiality categories: Cellular Lethal (CL), Developmental Lethal (DL), Subviable (SV), Viable with Phenotype (VP), and Viable with No Phenotype (VN). We then investigated the correlation between these categories and drug development success rates, as well as their side effect profiles.
