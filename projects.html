Functional Genomics and Drug Targets

This project focuses on using public genomic and phenotypic databases to better understand drug targets.

We used the International Mouse Phenotyping Consortium (IMPC) database to stratify drug targets into essentiality categories and study links between gene essentiality and drug responses. We applied the Full Spectrum of Intolerance to Loss-of-Function (FUSIL), proposed by Cacheiro et al. (2020), to group genes/targets into five categories: Cellular Lethal (CL), Developmental Lethal (DL), Subviable (SV), Viable with Phenotype (VP), and Viable with No Phenotype (VN). We then investigated associations with drug development success rates and side-effect profiles.

Genotype–Phenotype Correlations in Neurodevelopmental Disorders (NDDs)

© 2025 Genomics England

This project uses Genomics England’s rare-disease cohort to understand genetic correlations with specific phenotypes in patients with NDDs.

We investigate relationships between specific genetic variants and clinical presentations in NDDs. By analysing whole-exome sequencing data alongside detailed clinical phenotypes (described using HPO terms), we aim to identify novel genotype–phenotype correlations that can inform diagnosis and potential treatment strategies. This work improves our understanding of the genetic architecture of NDDs and supports personalised medicine.

Bioinformatics Pipelines and Python Utilities Development

© 2025 Hussein Kassir

Developed bioinformatics pipelines and Python utilities to streamline genomic data analysis workflows. Built Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) processing pipelines using Snakemake and GATK4, automated routine analyses on Linux, and created lightweight Python packages for tasks such as GC content calculation, reverse complement generation, and in-frame stop-codon detection.

These tools improved reproducibility, reduced manual workload, and enabled scalable processing of sequencing datasets across multiple projects.
